Visualization

A Visual and Interactive Stage Plot

The stage plot is a graphical representation of your installation, displayed in the main interface. Each fixture is represented by an icon whose color exactly reflects the DMX value sent in real time.

Without being on stage, you can tell at a glance which fixture is on and whether the groups are responding correctly. Organized into 6 groups (A to F), the plot can be adapted to your actual configuration.

Click a group to select it and apply color or intensity directly. The plot becomes an alternative control interface to the AKAI, StreamDeck or tablet, ideal during preparation phases.

Features

Everything to Visualize and Control Your Installation

🗺️

Fixture Groups

The stage plot organizes your fixtures into 6 groups (A to F) corresponding to classic stage lighting positions. Select an entire group with one click to apply color and intensity simultaneously.

🎯

Zone Control

Click a zone to select the group, then modify color and intensity from the adjacent sliders. More intuitive than abstract DMX sliders.

⚡

Real-time Feedback

Each icon's color is continuously updated based on actual DMX values, whether the source is the AKAI, AI Lighting or the timeline, at 25 fps.

🔧

Customizable Patch

DMX patch is fully customizable. MyStrow offers automatic patch (addresses 1, 11, 21…) that you can manually adjust to match your installation. Supports up to 4 Art-Net universes (2,048 channels). Patch is saved in the show file.

Lighting Architecture

MyStrow's 6 Fixture Groups

MyStrow organizes lighting into 6 groups (A to F) corresponding to standard stage lighting positions, for immediate setup.

A
Front Light
Front-of-house fixtures lighting artists from the front. Main face light, essential for readability and expression.
DMX: 1, 11, 21…
B
Downstage Wash 1
Overhead wash fixtures creating vertical modeling and depth. Shapes silhouette from above.
DMX: 31, 41…
C
Downstage Wash 2
Second wash zone for different lighting in stage depth. Ideal for shows with multiple playing areas.
DMX: 51, 61…
D
Downstage Wash 3
Third wash zone for deep stages or installations requiring full overhead coverage.
DMX: 71, 81…
E
Side Light
Side fixtures creating relief, dramatic shadows and sculpting effects on artists and sets.
DMX: 91, 101…
F
Back Light
Upstage back fixtures lighting artists from behind. Create silhouette effects and dramatic atmospheres.
DMX: 111, 121…
Practical Use

The Stage Plot in Real Situations

🔌

Setup & Cabling Phase

Click a group, send a bright color: if the fixture lights up, the address and cabling are correct. The visual patch is your diagnostic tool.

🎨

Lighting & Adjustments

Manipulate groups from the plot to test colors and combinations. The global view lets you see color balance and adjust levels per group without leaving the interface.

👁️

Show Monitoring

During the show, the plot serves as passive monitoring: a permanent summary view of the entire installation state, without distracting from the main control panel.

🖥️

Dedicated Second Screen

With two screens, open the plot full screen on the second monitor for a dedicated installation view, while the control interface occupies the first screen.
