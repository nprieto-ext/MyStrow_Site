Technology

How AI Lighting Analyzes Your Music

1

Real-time audio capture

AI Lighting captures the audio signal directly from your player output or from your computer's microphone. Analysis starts as soon as sound is detected, with no prior configuration.

2

Spectral analysis & BPM

The analysis engine breaks down the audio signal into frequency bands: bass (20–250 Hz), mids (guitars, vocals) and highs (drums, cymbals). BPM is calculated continuously by detecting rhythmic energy peaks.

3

Color & intensity generation

Based on dominant frequencies and overall signal energy, the AI generates color combinations coherent with the musical mood. An energetic track triggers warm, intense colors; a ballad produces cool, soft tones.

4

Automatic DMX output

Calculated values are sent directly to MyStrow's DMX engine and transmitted in real time over your Art-Net line. All your fixtures react to the music continuously, with no manual intervention.

Analyzed Parameters

  • Bass frequencies (20–250 Hz)
  • Mid frequencies (250 Hz – 4 kHz)
  • High frequencies (4 kHz – 20 kHz)
  • BPM and tempo (40–200 BPM)
  • Global RMS level
  • Energy variations (transitions, breaks)

AI Features

An Autonomous Lighting Brain for Your Stage

🤖

Autonomous stage control

In AI Lighting mode, MyStrow automatically handles all your DMX fixtures. Each of the 6 groups (A to F) receives individual instructions based on its scenic role. The AI differentiates ambient lighting from accents and effects.

🎵

BPM & rhythm analysis

The BPM engine synchronizes light effects to the music beats: flash on the kick, intensity variation on chords, light transitions locked to musical dynamics.

🎨

Intelligent color generation

The algorithm produces coherent color palettes that evolve throughout the track and change dramatically on major transitions (chorus, break, outro).

🛠️

Programming assistance

Launch a track, activate AI, record the generated sequences on your timeline. You get a programming base to refine keyframe by keyframe.

Comparison

AI Lighting or Manual Programming?

AI Lighting Mode

  • Ideal for shows without rehearsal
  • Instant reaction to live music
  • Zero preparation, plug in and play
  • Perfect for concerts, parties, spontaneous events
  • Show generation for inspiration
  • Autonomous management during the event

Manual Programming

  • Full control over every keyframe
  • Millisecond-precise synchronization
  • Ideal for theatre and rehearsed shows
  • Specific effects not generable by AI
  • Exportable and reproducible shows
  • Combinable with Play Light mode

MyStrow offers both approaches in the same software. Start in AI mode, switch to Manual or Program for moments that require precise lighting. All 4 modes can be switched instantly during the show.

Use Cases

When to Use AI Lighting?

🎸

Rock & Electronic Concert

Powerful bass, high tempo, variable energy: the AI generates flashes, strobes and color changes in phase with the music. Zero intervention during the set.

🎷

Jazz & Acoustic Music

The AI adapts its algorithms: smooth transitions, warm coppery palettes, moderate intensities for an enveloping atmosphere.

🕺

DJ Night & Club

The AI automatically follows transitions, drops and builds, allowing the stage manager to focus on other aspects while maintaining a coherent light show.

📺

TV Sets & Streaming

The AI maintains dynamic lighting during musical sequences without mobilizing a dedicated operator. Its consistency guarantees a professional result every take.
